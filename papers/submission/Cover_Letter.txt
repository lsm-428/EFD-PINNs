Cover Letter for Displays

Dear Editor,

We are pleased to submit our manuscript entitled "EFD3D: A Physics-Informed Neural Network for Electrowetting Display Pixel Simulation" for consideration for publication in Displays.

This work presents the first Physics-Informed Neural Network (PINN) framework specifically designed for electrowetting display (EWD) pixel simulation. The framework addresses three critical limitations of existing simulation approaches: (1) inability to efficiently handle step voltage transitions required for practical EWD operation, (2) reliance on idealized parameters rather than realistic device conditions, and (3) insufficient three-dimensional resolution for capturing complex flow patterns.

The key contributions of this work include:

1. A novel 6D Triad input representation [x, y, z, V_from, V_to, t_since] that enables a single trained model to handle arbitrary voltage step transitions from 0–30 V without retraining.

2. Physical parameters derived from actual EWD device specifications, ensuring predictions reflect real device behavior.

3. An asymmetric dynamic contact angle model based on experimental observations of voltage-up (second-order underdamped) and voltage-down (first-order exponential) responses.

4. A compact dual-branch architecture with only 35K trainable parameters that achieves volume conservation error below 1% while enabling inference in approximately 3 s on entry-level GPU hardware.

5. Extensive ablation studies (5 variants) validating that each physics constraint contributes to solution quality, demonstrating that lower loss is not synonymous with better physics.

6. Inverse design capability supporting gradient-based optimization of voltage waveforms for specific optical response profiles.

The framework demonstrates that meaningful PINN research is accessible even with limited computational resources (NVIDIA Quadro P2000 with 5 GB VRAM), making it suitable for industrial EWD design and optimization workflows. The source code and trained models are available as open-source resources.

We believe this work is well-suited for Displays as it directly advances simulation capabilities for electrowetting display technology, providing a practical tool for pixel design optimization and inverse design workflows.

This manuscript has not been published previously and is not under consideration for publication elsewhere. All authors have approved the manuscript and agree with its submission to Displays.

Thank you for your consideration. We look forward to your response.
