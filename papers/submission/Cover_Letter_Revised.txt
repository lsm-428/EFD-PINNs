Cover Letter for Displays

Dear Editor,

We are pleased to submit our manuscript entitled "EFD3D: A Physics-Informed Neural Network for Electrowetting Display Pixel Simulation" for consideration for publication in Displays.

This work presents the first Physics-Informed Neural Network (PINN) framework specifically designed for three-dimensional electrowetting display (EWD) pixel simulation. The framework addresses critical limitations in existing simulation approaches, including the inability to efficiently handle step voltage transitions required for practical EWD operation, reliance on idealized parameters rather than realistic device conditions, and insufficient three-dimensional resolution for capturing complex flow patterns.

The key contributions of this work include:

1. **First PINN Framework for EWD Simulation**: To our knowledge, this is the first demonstration that PINNs can be applied to simulate three-dimensional two-phase flow in electrowetting displays. The framework achieves comparable accuracy to traditional CFD methods (volume conservation error below 1%) while enabling rapid inference (≈3 seconds per query) after a single training phase.

2. **Comprehensive PINN Implementation**: The framework employs a novel 6D Triad input representation [x, y, z, V_from, V_to, t_since] that enables a single trained model to handle arbitrary voltage step transitions from 0-30 V without retraining. It features a dual-branch MLP architecture with only 34,661 parameters, physics-informed loss functions enforcing Navier-Stokes equations and VOF transport, and device-calibrated physical parameters validated against real EWD measurements.

3. **Engineering Infrastructure for EWD Design**: The framework establishes a practical engineering tool with interactive design capabilities, validated device-calibrated physics parameters, and a foundation for automated waveform optimization. Extensive ablation studies (6 variants compared) validate that each physics constraint contributes to solution quality.

Notably, this work demonstrates that meaningful PINN research is accessible even with limited computational resources (NVIDIA Quadro P2000 with 5 GB VRAM), making it suitable for industrial EWD design and optimization workflows. The source code and trained models are available as open-source resources.

We believe this work is well-suited for Displays as it directly advances simulation capabilities for electrowetting display technology, providing a practical tool for pixel design optimization and inverse design workflows. The manuscript represents eight months of development involving over 120 training experiments, multiple architectural iterations, and systematic debugging of physics constraints.

This manuscript has not been published previously and is not under consideration for publication elsewhere. All authors have approved the manuscript and agree with its submission to Displays.

Thank you for your consideration. We look forward to your response.
